Asking Good Questions

In order to ask a good question, you first must know what you are trying to ask. If you are unable to make sense of the question, then chances are the person answering the question won't either. I think the first thing you need to do in order to ask a good question is to research on your own on the web and keep track of the answer you find. Next, plan your questions by outlining your information goals and a sequence of related questions to help you follow the conversation.

Now, when asking a good question, it is important to be concise and specific. Include exactly what is causing the problem, all relevant source code, and how you encountered it.You should avoid asking "yes" or "no" questions and instead, ask open-ended question. By using an open-ended you can get insights and additional information you might not have known existed. Some words to avoid in a question are "would," "should," "is," "are," because they lead to yes or no. Questions with the words "who," "what," "where," "when," "how," or "why" lead to people giving some though to their answers.
