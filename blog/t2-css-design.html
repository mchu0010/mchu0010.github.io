Margin, Border, Padding

September 30, 2014

Margin, padding, and borders are the most commonly used properties for spacing out elements in CSS. These three properties all part of what’s known as a Box Model in CSS. The Box Model is represented like this: in the middle you have the content, surrounding the content you have the padding, surrounding the padding you have the border, and surrounding the border you have the margin. Margins and padding are very similar. Margin is the space that surrounds the element and padding sets the of space inside of a block element. An easier way to think of it would be the margin is simple the space outside of something while the padding is the space inside of something. The main difference between these two is that padding adds extra space inside the border, while margin add extra space outside the border. The border is the edge of the element, and it enables you to add a visible border around an area. Without the border, it’s difficult to tell the difference between the padding and margin.
