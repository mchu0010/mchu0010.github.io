Stereotype Threat

Bluegills Chicago - Phase 0 Week 6

October 26, 2014

Stereotype threat and our social awareness and understanding of it is important so that we as a group are all on the same field. As an Asian-American, our stereotype is the "Model Minority". At first glance, many people may consider it as a blessing: "you’re Asian, you’re so smart", or "you must do well, your parents force you to succeed". The notions associated with Asian Americans are typically ones surrounding the ideas of overachieving and highly successful minority group. However, it’s easy to get lost and blend in. Being Asian doesn’t mean being smart. It can be difficult to get the help you need because its assumed that you understand to a certain degree, and compared to your fellow Asians, its shameful to admit that sometimes you are not up to par. Although it is a little reversed in the concept, stereotype threat has appeared in my experience in the opposite way; it allows me to hide behind the impression that I know what I’m doing, even though sometimes I don’t. It’s important that we are able to address it early on both individually within ourselves and in others. Positive reinforcement about our work and an extra helping hand (or brain) will go a long way in battling stereotype threats. Being frank and honest about what I do and do not understand will help me overcome any symptoms of it and help me to move forward with full confidence.
