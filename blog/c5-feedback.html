Pairing and Giving Feedback

Bluegills Chicago - Phase 0 Week 5

October 19, 2014

Before I started this bootcamp, I knew that pair programming is very important to Dev Bootcamp because it helps foster collaboration. We’ve been pairing 2 to 4 times a week since the beginning, and today I am going to talk about my experience with pair programming.

So far, I’ve really enjoyed my pairing sessions that I’ve had with my fellow boots. All my pairing partners have been helpful and always asked if I understood the purpose of each method and how it works in general. It’s a great way to learn and it helped me look at solving the challenges from a different perspective for better or for worse. It’s nice to see how the others go about their challenges and pseudo codes than how I do it. Overall I think pairing is a great way to learn as long as you and partner are not afraid to ask questions and to make sure both are doing equal amount of work.

Another important part of pairing is giving and receiving good feedback. I’ve received many feedbacks and most have very positive and helpful. I learned that I tend to jump into a program without completing my pseudocode and also that I need to be more vocal about switching rolls. I’m very happy that I’ve received these great actionable feedback because now I can try to improve on both of those so that my partner and I can do equal amount work. Also some good feedback about how I pair is that, I always ask if my partner understand what we’re doing every time we use a new method. I think the most difficult part of feedbacks is giving good feedback. I have hard time writing good feedback because I always think that my partners are so good and that there’s nothing else I think they can improve on. I usually don’t have anything bad to say about them so it’s hard to give actionable feedback.
